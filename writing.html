The Avatar

Who I Write For

I write for a man I call Tony. Tony is my avatar — a fictional but realistic picture of the Christian husband and father I want to reach. He gives me a clear face, a clear heart, and a clear set of struggles to write toward.

Tony is 34 years old, married, and raising a son in 8th grade and a daughter in 6th grade. He came to faith young, grew serious about Christ in his youth, drifted in college, and now finds himself coming back to God with a mixture of hope and hesitation. He is not a caricature. He is the kind of man who loves his family, wants to do right, and still carries the weight of old choices that never really left him.

In college, Tony got involved in sexual compromise, pornography, drunkenness, drug experimentation, shoplifting, and a plagiarism incident he has never wanted anyone else to know about. Those chapters did not disappear just because time passed. They became part of the gallery in his heart — the place where shame hangs the pictures it wants to keep showing him. Now he is attending church again, his wife has come to Christ, and he senses God opening a door for him in the life of the church. He wants to step into something meaningful. He wants his life to count. But every step forward seems to awaken the old accusation: you can’t, because of what you did.

. . . And Satan Laughed!

That is why I write. I write as a fellow traveler — a man who understands what it is to wrestle with shame, remember failure, and still long for God to use what feels broken. I do not write to pretend the past never happened. I write to tell Tony that the past does not get the final word.

God Repurposes Skeletons.

While God loved!… He was quietly turning Tony’s worst chapters into raw material for his redemption story. I write to remind Tony that his failure is not stronger than grace, his shame is not stronger than his calling, and his skeleton is not wasted in the hands of God.

I also write to call Tony to live with a H.A.T. of Humility, Authenticity, Transparency, and to share his HIStory with courage and truth. That is the kind of life that turns pain into testimony and regret into redemptive purpose, a life of GRACE.

G.R.A.C.E. is

God’s

Redemptive

Approach

Concerning

Everyone

That is the heart behind my writing. God’s grace is not only for the polished, the proven, or the publicly clean. It is for Tony, too. It is for the man who thinks his past has ended his future. It is for the man who wants to do something significant for God, but cannot quite believe he still can. It is for the man who needs to know that redemption is not theory — it is God’s way.

Why Tony Matters

Tony is not just one man. He is a picture of many men. He represents the husband who wants to lead, the father who wants to set a better example, and the believer who wants to return without being haunted by what he left behind. He is a reminder that a man can be wounded, repentant, and hopeful all at once.

That is why I keep writing to Tony. Because when Tony finds grace, many men see a way home.
